Short Biography

I have had the privilege of being exposed to both worlds in the east and the west. I was born in the U.S. and assembled in Iran.  Consequently, I did all of my primary educations in Iran with a strong emphasis on mathematics and physics.  Then, my intuition and understanding of engineering and some biological concepts was further refined during my studies at American universities.  I received my B.S. in Bioengineering from University of California, Berkeley (2004), M.S. in Mechanical Engineering from Boston University (2009), and Ph.D. in the field of systems and control theory with applications in robotics under the supervisions of Sean B. Andersson and Calin Belta from Boston University (2012). Currently, I am conducting research in the field of robotics as a Post-Doctoral Research Associate under the guidance of Lydia Kavraki and Moshe Vardi at Rice University.

 

Research Interests

Generally, I am interested in both the theoretical and the application sides of my field of study.  Particularly, I am interested in abstraction and control of continuous and discrete nondeterministic and stochastic systems on the theoretical side.  From the application side, I enjoy applying control, game, and logic concepts and tools (including robots) on the biological systems.  My current research lies in formal approaches to planning and control of robot motion.  It involves abstraction of continuous stochastic systems into Markov models and control of such models using formal (temporal logic) languages.
